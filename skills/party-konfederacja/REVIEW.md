# Ethics Review: Konfederacja

**Profile file:** skills/party-konfederacja/SKILL.md
**Review date:** 2026-05-27
**Process:** LLM-pair review (progressive + conservative analyst personas)

## Reviewer A (Progressive Analyst)

The Konfederacja profile presents an ideologically coherent libertarian-nationalist profile without resorting to strawmanning. The immigration section is factual and policy-based — it describes the party's restrictionist position accurately without using dehumanizing language. The EU skepticism is grounded in sovereignty arguments rather than xenophobia.

**Issues identified:**

1. **Nationality vs. ethnicity distinction missing** — The profile combines libertarian and nationalist wings but does not clearly distinguish between national sovereignty advocacy (constitutionally legitimate) and ethnic nationalism (potentially harmful). An educational simulation user might conflate these. The profile should explicitly state that Konfederacja's nationalism is framed in terms of state sovereignty and cultural continuity, not ethnic hierarchy.

2. **Immigration framing**: The phrase "does not accept that immigration is economically necessary" states the party's position but reads as a flat empirical claim rather than a contested political stance. Should be framed as a position the party argues, not a fact.

**Harmful output risk:** Low. The Output Constraints section is robust. The red lines are clear. No content advocates violence or illegal activity.

## Reviewer B (Conservative Analyst)

The economic libertarian positions are internally consistent and accurately reflect the KORWiN/Nowa Nadzieja tradition. The flat tax and deregulation positions are the party's genuine flagship policies and are presented fairly. References to Hayek and Friedman are appropriate for the party's intellectual tradition.

**Issues identified:**

1. **Healthcare section accuracy**: The claim that "centralized healthcare systems" uniformly failed across the former Eastern Bloc overstates the case — several post-communist countries have performed comparably to Western Europe on health outcomes. The profile should frame this as the party's empirical argument, not an established fact.

2. **Immigration economic claim**: Same concern as Reviewer A — framing the labor shortage dismissal as a party position rather than an objective truth improves accuracy and reduces potential for misleading output.

**Harmful output risk:** Low-medium for the immigration section. The word "restrictionist" is accurate and neutral. No slurs, dehumanizing content, or violence advocacy found. Profile presents Konfederacja's positions as policy advocacy, not as moral imperatives about groups.
