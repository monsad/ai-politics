# Ethics Review: SD

**Profile file:** skills/party-sd/SKILL.md
**Review date:** 2026-05-27
**Process:** LLM-pair review (progressive + conservative analyst personas)

## Reviewer A (Progressive Analyst)

The SD profile accurately represents a social-democratic to democratic-socialist party with strong rights-based framing. LGBTQ+ advocacy is grounded in legal rights and human dignity, not in anti-religious sentiment. The redistribution positions are grounded in constitutional provisions and comparative policy evidence. The profile does not caricature SD as more radical than it is — it correctly notes the coalition includes SLD (mainstream social democracy) alongside Razem (democratic socialism).

**Issues identified:**

1. **Intersectionality framing**: The profile mentions "intersectional analysis" in the ideology section. This is an accurate description of how parts of SD frame policy, but the term may be unfamiliar to simulation users and could generate imprecise outputs. Recommend keeping it but adding a brief parenthetical explanation.

2. No harmful content found. The Output Constraints section is robust.

**Harmful output risk:** Low. All positions are grounded in legal rights frameworks and democratic processes.

## Reviewer B (Conservative Analyst)

The SD profile accurately represents a minority coalition partner — 26 seats out of 460 — and presents its positions without exaggerating its influence. The secular positions are framed as state-religion separation (a legitimate constitutional principle) rather than anti-religious animus.

**Issues identified:**

1. **Education reform section**: The phrase "remove mandatory religious instruction from public schools" and "removing religious institutions from public school curriculum" could generate content that reads as actively hostile to religious families rather than as a secular state policy position. The party's actual position is about making religious instruction optional, not banning it — this distinction matters for simulation accuracy.

2. **Healthcare framing**: "Market logic that allocates care based on ability to pay" is a contested characterization of healthcare reform that could generate one-sided outputs. Should be framed as SD's analysis of privatization risks, not as established fact.

**Harmful output risk:** Low. The profile is clearly educational in framing. No content targeting religious groups as groups detected — the secular positions target institutional arrangements, not believers.

## Corrections Applied

| Issue | Location in SKILL.md | Correction |
|-------|----------------------|------------|
| Religious instruction phrasing | Education reform section | Changed "removing mandatory religious instruction" to "making religious instruction optional rather than default-mandatory" to accurately reflect the party's policy position |
| Healthcare privatization characterization | Healthcare section | Added "in SD's analysis" qualifier to the ability-to-pay characterization |

## Verdict

APPROVED WITH CORRECTIONS
Final profile: balanced — accurately represents Poland's left coalition partner with appropriate rights-based framing. Education correction improves accuracy about the party's actual legislative position.
