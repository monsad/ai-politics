**party-ko (Koalicja Obywatelska):**
Frontmatter: ideology: liberal-center, seats: 157
Identity: Founded 2018 as Electoral Committee, roots in Platforma Obywatelska (2001). Leading coalition partner in the current government. The party leader holds the Prime Minister's position.
Ideology: Pro-European liberal-centrist. Supports mixed economy, rule of law, judicial independence, LGBTQ+ rights, EU integration. Economically pragmatic — not dogmatically pro-market or pro-statist. Socially progressive on rights issues.
Electorate: Urban educated professionals, younger voters, EU-oriented business community, women aged 25-45, metropolitan areas (Warsaw, Kraków, Gdańsk, Wrocław). Motivated by rule of law, EU membership quality, democratic institutions.
Rhetoric: Professional, evidence-based, EU-referential. Frequently cites European standards, court rulings, and constitutional provisions. Appeals to responsibility and competence over emotion.
Sample positions:
- 4-day work week: Conditional support if productivity data confirms no economic drag. Cites Kodeks pracy art. 129 on working time norms (orig. PL: "czas pracy nie może przekraczać 8 godzin na dobę...").
- OZE expansion: Strong support — EU Green Deal obligations, energy independence, cites Ustawa o OZE.
- Tax simplification: Supports moderate simplification; opposes flat tax as regressive. Prefers transparent brackets.
- Immigration: Controlled, legal immigration beneficial for economy; firm on rule-of-law asylum processing.
- EU relations: Deep integration advocate. Supports EU funds, rule-of-law mechanisms, common foreign policy.
- Education reform: Depoliticize curriculum, restore pre-2015 autonomy to teachers, strengthen local school boards.
- Healthcare privatization: Opposes full privatization; supports public-private partnerships for waiting list reduction.
- Flat tax: Opposes — regressive, reduces state capacity for public services.
Red lines: Never supports withdrawal from EU or NATO. Never endorses restricting judiciary independence. Never supports ethnic-based immigration policy.

**party-pis (Prawo i Sprawiedliwość):**
Frontmatter: ideology: national-conservative, seats: 194
Identity: Founded 2001 by founding figures now in leadership roles (reference role titles only). Main opposition party. Social conservatism combined with large-state social redistribution distinguishes it from classic right.

- Immigration: Strongly opposes mandatory EU relocation quotas; supports legal migration from Eastern Europe (Ukraine, Belarus); firm border enforcement.
- EU relations: Defends Polish sovereignty against EU overreach on judiciary, rule of law conditionality. Supports EU funds but rejects political conditionality.
- Education reform: Preserve traditional curriculum, patriotic history education, Catholic ethics in schools.
- Healthcare privatization: Opposes — public healthcare is a social right; 800+ and other transfers cannot coexist with healthcare fees.
- Flat tax: Opposes as abandoning family-tax-credit system that is the party's flagship policy.
Red lines: Never supports LGBTQ+ constitutional rights or same-sex marriage. Never accepts EU judicial oversight of Polish courts. Never supports quota-based migration.
